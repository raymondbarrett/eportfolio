the creation of the covid cascade simulation

At the heart of this model is a large undirected graph.
The idea is simple. If a matrix A can be constructed such that:
A(i,j) = A(j,i) = chance of either node i or j getting infected given that the other gets infected,
we can perform the multiplication Ax, and the resultant vector
will be the probability that each node gets infected on the next step.
The next challenge is generating A.

Currently, the model uses the idea of clustered communities inside residence halls,
meaning that each residence hall is split into floors, with a set chance of infection between floormates,
and each floor is split into rooms, with roommates having a set chance of infection between them.
The weights of the edges are different depending on the style of dorm; apartment-style dorms will have
a lower intra-floor spread probability compared to traditional-style
dorms with shared bathrooms.
Then, a set number of inter-residence hall communities are created.
This represents clubs, classes, or just going out with friends.
A set number of communities are created, each having a random community size based on a normal distribution
and a random intracommunity weight based on another normal distribution.
Finally, the last row is connected to every individual at the school and represents the chance of
an individual getting infected by someone from outside the institute.

Next, as an individual gets infected, a countdown gets triggered for that node, representing the delay
while that individual gets tested, or exhibits symptoms and is quarantined. When the countdown hits 0,
the individual is permanently removed from the system.
This draws heavily upon the SEIR model of disease modeling.
